Oceans are absorbing most of the excess heat trapped by greenhouse gases, leading to rising temperatures. This has severe consequences for marine life, coral reefs, and weather patterns. Warmer waters hold less oxygen, creating "dead zones" where marine life cannot survive. Coastal development, pollution, and climate change are destroying critical marine habitats like mangroves, seagrass beds, and coral reefs. Even worse the micro plastics can become denser in areas making more “dead zones”
